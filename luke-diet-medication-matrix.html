What Would Help Most

Ranked by expected impact.

⚠ Named Gap — Adjunctive Cardiac Medications

Standard ACVIM-aligned care for a dog at Luke's furosemide dose would include pimobendan (improves contractility so less diuretic is needed), an ACE inhibitor (reduces preload/afterload), and potentially spironolactone (supplemental diuretic allowing furosemide reduction). Luke has none of these. This is not a criticism of his care team — it may be a deliberate decision given his CKD (ACE inhibitors require monitoring in Stage 2 CKD) and his quality-of-life focus. But it is a gap worth naming explicitly to any new provider.

⚠ Priority 1 — High-dose fish oil · immediate, no Rx

Current: ~132 mg EPA+DHA/day. Target: ~1,440 mg/day minimum. Adding a liquid fish oil (e.g. Welactin Canine, ~1,080 mg per pump) to Luke's daily food bowl would close most of this gap in a single product. Expected benefits: reduced cardiac inflammation, anti-arrhythmic effect, attenuation of cardiac cachexia (muscle wasting), mild anti-inflammatory support for joints. No interactions with current medications. One of the single highest-value changes available.

⚠ Priority 2 — Caloric sufficiency review

Luke receives 4 patties/day × 352 kcal = 1,408 kcal/day. For a 35.9 kg dog with cardiac disease, the typical RER is ~930 kcal/day with a disease multiplier of 1.2–1.4 = ~1,116–1,302 kcal/day. At 1,408 kcal, Luke is meeting and slightly exceeding maintenance needs — this is appropriate given his cardiac cachexia risk. The ongoing weight loss (98 → 79.1 lb) is more likely attributable to diuresis and muscle wasting than caloric deficit. Continue monitoring weight monthly; if loss continues below 78 lb, discuss increasing to 4.5 patties or adding a calorie-dense topper.

⚠ Priority 3 — Confirm food phosphorus content

Contact Steve's Real Food directly for the absolute phosphorus content in mg per 8 oz patty. This is the missing piece for understanding Luke's IRIS phosphorus picture. If the food phosphorus is high, a phosphorus binder (given with meals) is a straightforward intervention that does not require changing the diet itself.

✓ Well-covered — Joint supplementation · keep Cosequin DS

With Cosequin DS back on board: glucosamine 3,050 mg/day, chondroitin 1,050 mg/day, MSM 1,150 mg/day. Chondroitin is the key ingredient here — it protects existing cartilage from enzymatic breakdown. Without Cosequin DS, chondroitin drops to just 150 mg/day from the Wuffes Joint chew alone, which is insufficient. Green-lipped mussel in the food adds further anti-inflammatory joint support. Stack is solid — maintain it.

✓ Already well-covered — Taurine

Steve's Pork Blocks contain added taurine (0.23%) plus naturally occurring taurine in pork heart. Combined with the Wuffes multivitamin, the dietary taurine picture is solid. A whole-blood taurine test would confirm sufficiency, but the diet is not obviously deficient.
