Where Luke is right now

Luke is still very active, but he's steadily slowed over the past two years — from pain, not lost enthusiasm. His back right leg is the sore one (both are sore, the right more so): he rises slowly, increasingly runs with both back legs moving as one, and on a startled quick turn the leg can give out — he's fallen. He's changed how he settles, too — he spins to swing his right leg under him and rest on the hip rather than bend the joint deeply, and sits with that leg off to the side.

He digs one arm at a time now, and much slower — rather than the fast back-and-forth digging he used to do, like he was swimming. He gets winded faster, needing more breaks. I occasionally hear a toenail drag on a step. I want to slow and reverse his decline as much as I can.

As of July 2026, I'm getting him set up with a Walking Wheels cart — I now have all the parts, pieces, and straps. He'll enjoy the walks at speed, without the pain in his knee, hip, and ligaments.
