★The goal

What Rikki is asking for

A food plan that helps Luke maintain what can be maintained, keeps his body fed, helps him feel healthy, and gives him everything he needs to thrive — within what his heart and kidneys allow. The aim is good days and quality of life, not aggressive life-extension: Luke's stated goals of care are comfort and a peaceful trajectory (DNR, no hospitalization). Nutrition is the lever his owner most wants to pull for him now.

Who Luke is: friendly with everyone · a brilliant, athletic beast · a lover of food and learning. His food motivation is very high — a genuine asset for compliance.

2Before formulating — worth confirming

Several load-bearing facts are soft. The owner is open to these (vet/specialist-led) so the plan is built on reality, not assumption:

  • Whole-blood taurine level Moderate — Weimaraner is a DCM-predisposed breed, the cardiac diagnosis is empiric, and taurine has never been measured. If low, taurine-responsive DCM can partly reverse. Cheap; test before assuming.
  • Fresh renal panel + phosphorus + urinalysis/UPC on a clean, hydrated sample Moderate — to true up the IRIS stage before protein/phosphorus decisions (no UA since 2023; most chem is ~10 months old).
  • Echocardiogram / NT-proBNP Strong value — confirm CHF and distinguish valve disease from DCM; changes dietary priorities (taurine/carnitine emphasis).
  • ALT work-up — carprofen can injure the liver; understand the rising ALT before adding a liver-support nutraceutical that could mask it.

3The constraints the plan must respect

The central problem is that cardiac and renal diets pull in opposite directions, and no single shelf diet fits — which is exactly why a custom, nutritionist-built plan is the right call.

Cardiac (Stage D) + renal (IRIS 2) — the tension

  • Protein: ACVIM guidance is to avoid low-protein renal diets unless severe renal failure is present. Luke is Stage 2 (not severe), so the literature favors moderate high-quality protein, not restriction — protect his muscle.
  • Phosphorus: his is 5.1 (Sep '25), above the IRIS Stage-2 target of ~2.7–4.6. The literature's pivotal move is controlling phosphorus with an intestinal binder (titrated to serum phosphorus) rather than by crushing protein.
  • Sodium: moderate, not extreme — over-restriction can activate RAAS and worsen renal perfusion at his diuretic dose. A hidden-sodium audit is the easy win (see the med vehicle + snacks below).
  • Omega-3 (EPA/DHA): the literature's clearest win for all three organs (renal, cardiac, joint) and possibly NSAID-sparing. His intake is ~132 mg/day ≈ 6% of a cardiac target (~2,300 mg) Moderate — a large, safe headroom.
  • Moisture: keep it high — supports hydration on a diuretic.
  • GDV / meal pattern: deep-chested breed → small, frequent meals, no vigorous activity around meals.

4What Luke eats now — exactly

Primary food — currently in flux (May 2026)

His long-standing base was Steve's Real Food Pork blocks, raw · 4/day (~1,408 kcal), sous-vided to 151 °F overnight into a warm "soup" fed in quarters with added water (he took in a lot of water this way). Steve's has since pulled the pork blocks, and the diet has been unstable: Whitefish formula (he lost weight on it), then Beef blocks (he won't clear the bowl — very out of character), and now pork nuggets (arrived May 2026). Rikki's explicit goal is a predictable, maximally-nutritious diet to end the chaos. Two signals for the DACVN: (1) the raw pork/shellfish base is a poor fit for this dog — phosphorus/protein unrestricted, uncontrolled sodium, pathogen risk on chronic NSAID — so a cooked, reformulated diet that keeps the moisture is the key question; (2) the ~20 lb loss over the past year with new bowl-refusal, "boniness," and reduced strength needs a calorie/protein target that protects lean mass (cardiac-cachexia watch). Carrots — his ritual (do not remove)

Unlimited raw, peeled carrots throughout the day, self-served from a "snack board." This is also his household quality-of-life barometer — a drop in carrot enthusiasm precedes decline. Relevant to formulate around: fiber, natural sugars, water, and potassium at an unmeasured daily volume.

Snack board & favorites

Dairy (yogurt, cottage cheese, cheese — his favorites), plus bananas, Gala apples, tomatoes, frozen blueberries, softened ice cubes, peanut butter, sweet potatoes, rice, pancakes, and owner-made frozen treats. Owner already notes apples/bananas/sweet potato are sugary and worth limiting. Dairy & peanut butter are hidden-sodium/phosphorus sources worth quantifying.

Med delivery vehicle

Since 2024, all medications — furosemide q12h and every other pill — are given in a small ball of goat cheese. His food motivation makes med delivery reliable. Worth accounting for in his fat, dairy, sodium, and phosphorus budget (soft cheeses carry both sodium and phosphorus), though the daily volume used for pilling is small.

6Questions for the nutritionist

  • Should Luke move off the raw pork/shellfish diet to a cooked, custom formulation — and can it keep the high moisture he benefits from?
  • How best to control phosphorus (which binder, titrated how) without over-restricting protein at his Stage 2?
  • What protein level & sources best protect his muscle (cachexia risk) while respecting the kidney — low-phosphorus proteins like egg white / whey?
  • What omega-3 (EPA+DHA) target dose and source, given the ~94% gap, and can it help spare his carprofen?
  • What sodium target is right — and how do we account for the bread med-vehicle, dairy, and peanut butter?
  • Does he need taurine / L-carnitine (test first), and any renal-safe adjustments to his current supplement stack?
  • How do we keep his unlimited carrots and dairy snacks in the plan (they're QoL anchors) while accounting for their sugar, potassium, sodium, and phosphorus?
  • Meal pattern & calories for a Stage-D heart + GDV-risk breed at ideal BCS — small frequent meals, and the right daily kcal to hold weight?

7What to build around (quality-of-life anchors)

Given the goal is thriving and good days, please try to formulate around these rather than removing them:

  • Carrots — unlimited, self-served, and his QoL barometer. Central to his day and his joy.
  • His food motivation — very high; use it. It's why med compliance and diet transitions are realistic for him.
  • The snack ritual & dairy — sources of comfort and routine; quantify and moderate rather than eliminate where possible.
  • High moisture & palatability — keep meals appealing and hydrating; appetite itself is a QoL and cardiac signal for him.
