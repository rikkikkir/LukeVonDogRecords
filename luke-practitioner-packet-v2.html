What Rikki wants for Luke

Luke is 12, in the later chapter of two conditions whose treatments conflict. An end-of-life planning conversation was opened with the Thornwood team on May 14, 2025 — this is a framework, not an imminent-decision document. It exists so a stranger can make the choice Rikki would make.

Who Luke is: friendly with everyone · a brilliant, athletic beast · a lover of food and learning.

Signals the household watches
  • Carrot enthusiasm — the daily QoL barometer; a shift in interest precedes decline by hours to days. [baseline: Rikki to add normal range]
  • Resting respiratory rate — target < 30/min fully asleep (baseline being established at home).
  • Willingness to engage — greets, follows Rikki, responds to routine.
What a crisis looks like
  • Resting-asleep breaths > 30/min, or increased effort
  • Open-mouth breathing or blue-gray gums — emergency
  • Non-productive retching + distended abdomen — GDV emergency
  • Sudden collapse or severe lethargy
  • Refusal to eat 24 h+ in a food-motivated dog
What Rikki needs from providers
  • An explicit framework — when the conversation shifts from management to transition planning.
  • No surprises — she carries Sam's death as context and won't repeat an unprepared crisis.
  • Honesty about trajectory — she can hold hard information and prefers it to uncertainty.
Rikki's wishes for a crisis or the end — her directive; please honor it

Luke is to have a peaceful, dignified death. His health and comfort come before resuscitation.

  • DNR — no CPR.
  • No hospitalization.
  • Keep him well and comfortable for as long as that is possible; when his time comes, let him go peacefully — do not attempt to resuscitate or prolong.
A few specifics Rikki may add when she's ready: whether, in a breathing crisis, to ease his distress with oxygen or light sedation for comfort (which is not resuscitation) while she reaches him; any financial ceiling; and who may authorize comfort care if she is briefly unreachable while traveling.
